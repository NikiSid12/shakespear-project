Interesting facts about Shakespeare

Little-known details about the life and work of the great playwright

Facts from the life of Shakespeare

The exact date of birth is unknown

Shakespeare was baptized on April 26, 1564. It is generally accepted that he was born on April 23, St. George's Day, but there is no documentary evidence to support this.

Primary school education

Shakespeare attended Stratford Grammar School, where he studied Latin and classical literature. He did not receive a higher education, which gave rise to the "Shakespeare question."

A strange marriage

Shakespeare married Anne Hathaway when he was 18 and she was 26. The marriage was hastily concluded: after receiving a marriage license on November 27, 1582, the wedding took place on November 28..

A successful businessman

Shakespeare was not only a playwright but also a successful businessman. He owned a stake in the Globe Theatre, bought property in Stratford and London, and lent money at interest.

Curse on the grave

Shakespeare's tombstone in Holy Trinity Church, Stratford, bears the curse: "Friend, for God's sake, do not dig up the remains this earth has taken; he that toucheth not is blessed for ever, and cursed is he that toucheth my ashes."

Shakespeare and the English language

Inventor of words

Shakespeare introduced more than 1,700 words into the English language that we use today. Among them: "addiction," "bedroom," "eyeball," "fashionable," and "lonely."

Author of popular phrases

Many phrases we use today first appeared in Shakespeare's works: "to be or not to be," "all the world's a stage," "all that glitters is not gold," "a lady protests too much."

Shakespeare's Dictionary

Shakespeare's vocabulary is estimated at 17,000-29,000 words (by comparison, the average educated person uses about 3,000-5,000 words). The King James Version uses about 8,000 different words.

Different spelling options

Shakespeare's surname appears in documents with over 80 spelling variations, including Shakespear, Shakspear, Shakspere, and even Shaxberd. The playwright himself signed his name differently in different documents.

Riddles and hoaxes

The Shakespearean Question

Some scholars doubt that a man named William Shakespeare of Stratford wrote all the works attributed to him. Francis Bacon, Christopher Marlowe, and Edward de Vere have been suggested as possible authors.

The Mystery of Portraits

Не существует прижизненных портретов Шекспира, написанных с натуры. Самый известный портрет — гравюра Мартина Друшаута из Первого фолио (1623). Также существует Чандосовский портрет, но его атрибуция спорна.

Lost Years

Nothing is known about Shakespeare's life from 1585 to 1592 (between the birth of the twins and his arrival in London). This period is known as the "Lost Years." Various theories exist: he was a teacher, a soldier, a lawyer, or traveled throughout Europe.

The Curse of Macbeth

There's a superstition in the theater that uttering the title of "Macbeth" in the theater brings bad luck. Actors call it "the Scottish play." Shakespeare is believed to have used actual witchcraft in the play.

Legacy and influence

Record-breaking film adaptations

Shakespeare's works have been adapted for the screen over 400 times—a record for any writer. "Hamlet" has been adapted for the screen over 50 times, and "Romeo and Juliet" over 40 times. The first adaptation was made in 1899.

Shakespeare in Space

On Mercury, there is a crater called Shakespeare, 400 km in diameter. An asteroid (2985 Shakespeare) and craters on Uranus's moons, Miranda and Oberon, are also named after Shakespeare.

Shakespeare Day

April 23 is celebrated as Shakespeare Day, the date Shakespeare died on (according to the Julian calendar). April 23 is also World Book and Copyright Day..

Monuments around the world

Shakespeare monuments have been erected in London, Stratford, New York, San Francisco, Melbourne, Buenos Aires, and many other cities around the world. New York's Central Park boasts the only Shakespeare monument depicting him seated.

Funny and curious facts

Shakespeare Brewery

In 2016, to mark the 400th anniversary of Shakespeare's death, a British brewery released "Shakespeare's Birthplace," a beer brewed according to a 16th-century recipe that included barley, hops, and wild rosemary.

Shakespeare's Cat

Shakespeare's works mention over 50 different animals, but never cats. However, the plays also include references to tigers, lions, and leopards..

The Ghost in Hamlet

The ghost of Hamlet's father is one of the most famous ghosts in world literature. Interestingly, in the original text, the ghost speaks 90 lines, but in some productions, he speaks nothing at all.

Shakespeare and medicine

Shakespeare's works mention over 700 medical terms and describe 64 different diseases. Some researchers believe Shakespeare may have worked as an apothecary or physician.

A photograph of Shakespeare's "ghost"

In 1869, a photograph was taken that purported to capture Shakespeare's ghost. In reality, it was a double exposure. The photograph became popular and gave rise to numerous legends.
